Florian Orgeret

As an evolutionary ecologist, my main focus is on ontogeny, movement ecology, and dispersal. I specialize in analyzing extensive datasets from biologging and tracking devices deployed on juvenile animals. My main research aims to better understand the development of individual variation with age and its potential impact on metapopulation dynamics, especially in relation to global change.Â 

My career is characterized by a dedication to continuous learning and the promotion of knowledge exchange through organizing regular, informal hands-on workshops. These sessions enable me not only to pass on new analysis techniques, but also to encourage participants from different institutions to make their own contributions. I am deeply convinced that this mutual sharing of knowledge is essential to our collective advancement and progress!

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Swiss Ornithological Institute's Ecological Research Unit, working on the red kite project up to the end of 2024.
